I am a C.V. Starr Postdoctoral Fellow at the Princeton Neuroscience Institute, working under the joint supervision of Annegret Falkner and Mala Murthy.

My graduate work with Jonathan Whitlock was completed in 2019 at the Kavli Institute for Systems Neuroscience/CNC in Norway. We used a combination of electrophysiology, optogenetics and computational methods to study how associative cortices encode body posture in freely moving rats. A part of this project was published in Science.

Nervous systems have evolved to solve a myriad of existential queries, bestowing the curse of infuriating complexity on the field of neuroscience. I am, nonetheless, fundamentally curious about animal behavior, both how it is persistently affected by periodic bouts of cellular activity and how it, in turn, shapes the intricate neuronal dynamics on sub-second scales. If interested further, check out my introductory discussion of this topic.
